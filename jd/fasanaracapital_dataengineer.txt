The role

We are looking for a hands-on Data Engineer / Backend Engineer to help build and evolve the data infrastructure that supports our credit and broader investment businesses. This role will sit at the intersection of data engineering, backend development, and business problem-solving, with a strong focus on reliability, scalability, and enabling better data access across the firm. You'll work closely with data scientists, engineers, and business stakeholders to design practical solutions that improve how we ingest, process, transform, and use data. A genuine interest in credit markets and financial data will help you thrive in the role.

Responsibilities

Design, build, and maintain robust data pipelines to ingest, process, and transform structured and unstructured data from multiple sources
Develop and manage ETL workflows to ensure data is delivered accurately and on time to target systems
Build and improve backend services, APIs, and data workflows that support the wider platform
Monitor data quality, availability, and reliability, and proactively resolve issues as they arise
Help evolve the data platform into clear, self-service tools that enable teams across the business to access and use data independently
Work closely with data scientists, engineers, and business teams to understand requirements and deliver practical data solutions
Build knowledge across a range of credit asset classes to support data modelling and business insights
 Leverage AI coding tools (Claude, etc.) for brainstorming and development, as these tools have become critical for productivity


Requirements

Strong hands-on experience in data engineering, backend development, or a closely related technical role
Proficiency in Python, with additional experience in at least one other programming language such as Go, Java, C#, or C++
Strong SQL skills and good working knowledge of relational databases, ideally MySQL
Experience building data pipelines, APIs, and data ingestion processes from external sources
Familiarity with Git/GitLab and modern development practices
Interest in finance, financial data, or credit markets
Strong communication skills, curiosity, and a proactive, problem-solving mindset
Ability to show previous work through projects, repositories, or other tangible examples
